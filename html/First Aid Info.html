First Aid Basics

Here you can find information about essential first aid techniques and procedures.

CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)
  • If someone is unresponsive and not breathing normally, call 911 immediately.
  • Start chest compressions by pushing hard and fast in the center of the chest (at least 2 inches deep) at a rate of 100-120 compressions per minute.
  • If you're trained, combine chest compressions with rescue breaths (30 compressions followed by 2 breaths) until help arrives.
Choking
  • If someone is choking and can't cough, speak, or breathe, perform the Heimlich maneuver procedure:
    1. Stand behind the person, place a fist just above their navel, and grasp it with your other hand. Pull sharply inward and upward.
    2. Continue this until the object is dislodged or the person loses consciousness (then start CPR).
Bleeding
  • For minor cuts, wash the wound with soap and water, apply an antiseptic, and cover with a sterile bandage.
  • For severe bleeding, apply direct pressure with a clean cloth or bandage and elevate the injured area.
  • If bleeding doesn't stop, call 911.
Burns
  • For minor burns, run cool (not cold) water over the affected area for at least 10 minutes.
  • Cover with a sterile dressing or clean cloth.
  • For severe burns, call 911 and avoid using adhesive bandages or ointments.
Fractures and Sprains
  • Immobilize the injured area by using a splint or a rolled-up newspaper and wrap it with a bandage or cloth.
  • Keep the injured limb elevated if possible.
  • Seek medical attention.
Heart-Related Illnesses
  • For heat exhaustion, move to a cooler place, drink water, and rest.
  • For heatstroke, call 911. Move to a cooler place, use cold compresses, and remove excess clothing.
Poisoning
  • Call the Poison Control Center at 1-800-222-1222 immediately if you suspect poisoning.
  • Follow their guidance on what to do next.
Seizures
  • Keep the person safe by moving objects away from them
  • Place the person on their side after the seizure stops to prevent choking on saliva or vomit.
  • Do not restrain the person or put anything in their mouth.

Medical Guidelines

Learn about important medical guidelines and health recommendations.

  • Healthy Diet and Nutrition: Maintain a balanced diet with plenty of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and limited sugar, salt, and saturated fats.
  • Adequate Hydration: Drink enough water daily to stay well-hydrated. The recommended amount varies but is typically around 8-10 cups (2-2.5 liters) per day.
  • Adequate Sleep: Aim for 7-9 hours of quality sleep per night for optimal physical and mental health.
  • Regular Health Check-ups: Schedule regular health check-ups with your healthcare provider for preventive care and early detection of health issues.
  • Immunizations: Stay up to date with recommended vaccinations to prevent serious diseases.
